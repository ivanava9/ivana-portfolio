Hospital Performance Review

Built an executive Tableau dashboard that analyzes hospital revenue, admissions, and operational efficiency across branches and departments, enabling leadership to identify revenue drivers and prioritize performance improvement initiatives.

Project Overview

Summary / Context

Developed an executive performance dashboard for a multi-branch hospital network in Java to help leadership understand revenue drivers, monitor operational performance, and support data-driven decisions on resource allocation and growth strategies.

Goals

Build an interactive CEO dashboard that enables users to assess overall business performance, compare branches and departments, identify factors driving revenue variations, and investigate operational inefficiencies through drill-down analysis.

Process

Designed a Tableau dashboard by integrating six relational datasets covering admissions, billing, branches, departments, doctors, and patients. Defined financial and operational KPIs, selected suitable visualizations, implemented interactive filters, and translated analytical findings into business recommendations for executive decision-making.

Output

Delivered an interactive dashboard containing KPI scorecards, branch performance maps, revenue trends, payment analysis, referral source contributions, department efficiency analysis, and doctor performance metrics. The dashboard revealed that revenue was concentrated in Jakarta Selatan, Jakarta Utara, and Surabaya, while Insurance generated the highest pending payments and Oncology emerged as the highest-performing department, leading to recommendations on branch optimization, insurance claim processing, and partnership strategy improvements.

Key Achievements

Designed and delivered an executive dashboard integrating 6 relational datasets, enabling branch, department, and revenue performance analysis from a single reporting interface.
Developed 5 KPI scorecards covering revenue, admissions, revenue per admission, revenue per day, and trend monitoring to support executive performance reviews.
Built interactive analyses across 5,000 admissions and Rp98.866B total net revenue, allowing executives to investigate revenue drivers and operational efficiency.
Identified that 45% of admission bills were concentrated between Rp5M and Rp20M, highlighting revenue dependence on mid-range patient spending and opportunities for revenue optimization.
