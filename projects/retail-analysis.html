Retail Sales Performance Analysis

Analyzed grocery transaction data using SQL and BigQuery to identify revenue drivers, evaluate pricing effectiveness, and uncover customer purchasing patterns across product categories.

Project Overview

Summary / Context

Conducted an exploratory sales analysis for a multi-location grocery retailer to understand category performance, customer purchasing patterns, and pricing effectiveness. The project aimed to identify revenue drivers and provide data-driven recommendations for improving profitability and customer engagement.

Goals

Identify the highest-performing product categories, analyze the relationship between revenue and sales drivers such as units sold and customer count, and evaluate whether pricing strategies influence purchasing behavior and category performance.

Process

Performed data validation and preparation in BigQuery, integrated transaction, product, category, and customer tables, wrote SQL queries to answer business questions, analyzed category-level performance metrics, and translated findings into pricing, marketing, and product portfolio recommendations.

Output

Delivered an end-to-end sales performance analysis that identified Confections as the highest-performing category, contributing 12.87% of total revenue with a 99.85% repeat purchase rate. The analysis showed that customer spending behavior had no strong relationship with average unit price and that revenue was broadly distributed across categories and customers, leading to recommendations around category investment, basket-size growth, value-based pricing, and targeted marketing initiatives.

Key Achievements

Validated and prepared 4 integrated datasets and identified 67,526 transactions with null sales dates, ensuring data quality before analysis.
Analyzed category-level sales performance and identified Confections contributing 12.87% of total revenue, establishing it as the company's primary revenue driver.
Evaluated customer loyalty metrics and found that Confections achieved a 99.85% repeat purchase rate, indicating consistently strong repurchase behavior.
Investigated pricing and purchasing behavior and discovered that the highest-priced category, Grain, had the lowest unique buyers, demonstrating a weak relationship between price and customer demand.
